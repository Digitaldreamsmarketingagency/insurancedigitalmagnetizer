Have diabetes? Nothing to worry about even if you are travelling to places far and wide. Just follow the simple tips stated below to make your stay tension free! If you make the right preparations you should be able to minimize any potential problems and have an enjoyable and a safe trip. Your care routine should travel with you, especially if you have diabetes.

Staying Healthy!

  • Changes in what you eat, activity levels and time zones can affect your blood glucose.
  • Check levels often.
  • Consult your physician before increasing physical activity as travelling will involve more walking.
  • Wash hands often with soap and water.
  • Protect your feet. Specially be careful of hot pavements by pools and hot sand on beaches. Never go barefoot.
  • Keep your immunization records up-to-date.

Don't forget your medication:

  • Pack twice the amount of medicines you expect to need in case of travel delays.
  • Keep snacks, glucose gel or tablets in case your blood glucose drops.
  • Remember to keep your health insurance card and emergency phone numbers handy which includes your doctor's number.
  • Keep time zone changes in mind so that you know when to take your medicines.
  • If you use insulin, make sure you have a pack of glucagon emergency kit.
  • Find out where to get medical care if needed when away from home.

While travelling On the road:

  • Reduce your risk of blood clots by moving around every hour or two.
  • Pack smaller portions of snacks while travelling, such as fresh fruit, salads, dry fruits or low - fat yogurt.
  • Carry a few bottles of water.

Travelling by air:

  • Place all the diabetes supplies in the carry-on luggage. Keep medications and snacks at your seat for easy access. Don't store them in overhead bins or checked luggage.
  • If a meal is to be served during your flight, call ahead for a diabetic, low fat or low cholesterol meal. Wait until your food is about to be served before you take your insulin.
  • If the airline does not offer a meal, carry a nutritious meal yourself.
  • When drawing up your dose of insulin, do not inject air into the bottle as the ait in the flight will probably be pressurized.
  • Reduce your risk of blood clots by moving around every hour or two.
